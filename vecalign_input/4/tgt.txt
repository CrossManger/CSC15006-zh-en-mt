Two additional variables can influence the national system in important ways, and are necessary to complete the theory. 
These are chance and government. 
Chance events are developments outside the control of firms (and usually the nation’s government), such as pure inventions, breakthroughs in basic technologies, wars, external political developments, and major shifts in foreign market demand.They create discontinuities that can unfreeze or reshape industry structure and provide the opportunity for one nation’s firms to supplant another’s.
